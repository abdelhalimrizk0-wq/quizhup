1. Which of the following conditions is NOT included in the spectrum of Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS)?

Rationale: Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS) includes unstable angina, NSTEMI, and STEMI. Stable angina is a chronic condition with predictable pain on exertion, not part of the acute spectrum.

2. A patient presents with chest pain that occurs at rest and has increased in frequency and duration. This clinical presentation is most consistent with:

Rationale: Unstable angina is characterized by unexpected chest pain or discomfort that usually occurs while at rest, is more intense, and lasts longer (often >20 minutes) than stable angina.

3. Which of the following is considered a non-modifiable risk factor for cardiovascular disease?

Rationale: Non-modifiable risk factors are those that cannot be changed, such as age, gender, and family history. Smoking, cholesterol levels, and activity are modifiable.

4. Variant (Prinzmetal's) angina is primarily caused by:

Rationale: Variant angina is the result of coronary artery spasm and usually occurs at rest, often between midnight and 8:00 am.

5. The nurse is assessing a patient with chest pain using the NOPQRST acronym. What does 'P' stand for?

Rationale: In the NOPQRST assessment tool, 'P' stands for Precipitating and Palliative factors (what makes the pain worse or better).

6. Which cardiac biomarker is considered the preferred marker for diagnosing myocardial necrosis due to its high specificity?

Rationale: A cardiac-specific troponin (T or I) is the preferred marker to obtain in all patients with chest discomfort consistent with ACS as it has replaced CK-MB for diagnosing myocardial necrosis.

7. A patient with suspected ACS has a normal initial 12-lead ECG. What is the most appropriate nursing action?

Rationale: A normal ECG does not exclude the possibility of ACS. The patient requires serial ECGs, cardiac markers, and observation.

8. In the management of angina, what is the primary mechanism of action of Nitroglycerin?

Rationale: Nitroglycerin is a vasodilator that reduces myocardial oxygen demand by decreasing ventricular preload via vasodilation and improving arterial flow to ischemic areas.

9. NCLEX Style: A patient is prescribed sublingual nitroglycerin for angina. What instruction should the nurse provide regarding administration?

Rationale: Nitroglycerin should be taken 5 minutes apart (no more than 3 doses in 15 minutes). If pain persists, immediate care is needed. Tablets must be kept in a dark glass bottle, not plastic or clear containers.

10. Morphine sulfate is indicated for ACS patients when:

Rationale: Morphine is recommended for patients whose symptoms are not relieved after three sublingual nitroglycerin tablets or who have recurring symptoms despite therapy.

11. Which medication is an antiplatelet agent administered as soon as ACS is suspected?

Rationale: Aspirin (160 to 325 mg chewed) inhibits platelet aggregation and should be administered as soon as unstable angina or NSTEMI/STEMI is suspected.

12. Beta-blockers are used in ACS management primarily to:

Rationale: Beta-blockers reduce myocardial oxygen demand by decreasing heart rate, systemic arterial pressure, and myocardial contractility.

13. NCLEX Style: An elderly patient presents with confusion, weakness, and dyspnea but denies chest pain. The nurse should suspect:

Rationale: Older patients often present with atypical symptoms such as dyspnea, confusion, weakness, or fainting rather than typical substernal chest pain.

14. What ECG change indicates the "Zone of Injury" in an acute MI?

Rationale: The zone of injury is characterized by cells that do not fully repolarize due to deficient blood supply, recorded on ECG as ST-segment elevation.

15. Pathologic Q waves on an ECG generally indicate:

Rationale: Evidence of the zone of infarction (dead muscle) is seen as new pathologic Q waves, which reflect a lack of depolarization from the affected cardiac surface.

16. What is the optimal time frame for initiating fibrinolytic therapy after the onset of symptoms?

Rationale: Fibrinolytic therapy provides maximal benefit if given within the first 3 hours after the onset of symptoms. The "door-to-needle" goal is 30 minutes upon ED arrival.

17. Which of the following is an ABSOLUTE contraindication for fibrinolytic therapy?

Rationale: Suspected aortic dissection, along with prior intracranial hemorrhage or known intracranial neoplasm, are absolute contraindications. Peptic ulcer and anticoagulant use are relative contraindications.

18. True or False: Myocardial tissue is best salvaged if revascularization occurs within the first 120 minutes (2 hours) of symptom onset.

Rationale: Myocardial tissue can best be salvaged within the first 2 hours (120 minutes) after the onset of anginal symptoms.

19. Which heart sound is associated with decreased left ventricular compliance and is heard in almost all patients with MI?

Rationale: An S4 heart sound is heard in almost all patients with MI because of decreased left ventricular compliance. S3 indicates systolic dysfunction/failure.

20. Which of the following is a mechanical complication of Acute Myocardial Infarction?

Rationale: Mechanical complications include mitral valve regurgitation, left ventricular free wall rupture, ventricular septal rupture, and aneurysm. Shock is hemodynamic; Pericarditis is pericardial; VT is arrhythmic.

21. NCLEX Style: A patient with STEMI is receiving fibrinolytic therapy. Which finding would alert the nurse to a possible major complication?

Rationale: A change in LOC could indicate an intracranial hemorrhage, the most serious complication of fibrinolytic therapy. Minor oozing and reperfusion arrhythmias are expected or less critical.

22. What is the target LDL cholesterol level for patients with UA/NSTEMI initiated on statin therapy?

Rationale: Titration to a dose necessary to sustain an LDL cholesterol concentration of 70 mg/dL or lower is reasonable for ACS patients.

23. During an anginal episode, what physical assessment finding might indicate hypercholesterolemia?

Rationale: Xanthomas are yellow nodules or plaques on the skin that indicate hypercholesterolemia.

24. What is the correct dosage and administration route for Aspirin in the initial management of suspected MI?

Rationale: The recommended dose is 160 to 325 mg chewed to facilitate rapid absorption and inhibition of platelet aggregation.

25. If a patient requires Primary PCI (Percutaneous Coronary Intervention), the goal is to open the culprit vessel within how many minutes of arrival?

Rationale: If primary PCI is to be done, the goal is to open culprit vessels within 90 minutes of arrival ("door-to-balloon" time).

26. Which of the following patients is most likely to experience "Silent Ischemia" or atypical symptoms?

Rationale: Patients with diabetes, along with women and the elderly, are more likely to present with atypical symptoms or silent ischemia.

27. Which diagnostic test is the definitive method to diagnose coronary artery disease and anatomical blockages?

Rationale: Coronary angiography is an invasive diagnostic test that provides a definitive diagnosis of coronary artery disease by visualizing the anatomy and blockages.

28. The nurse observes T-wave inversion on the ECG of a patient with chest pain. This finding most likely represents:

Rationale: The outer region of the infarcted area is the zone of ischemia, where repolarization is impaired, manifesting as T-wave inversion.

29. Which medication should be discontinued if the patient's systolic blood pressure falls below 90 mmHg or heart rate drops below 50 bpm?

Rationale: Nitroglycerin causes vasodilation and can worsen hypotension. It should be withheld if SBP < 90 mmHg or HR < 50 bpm or > 100 bpm.

30. Dressler's syndrome is a complication of MI characterized by:

Rationale: Dressler's syndrome is a form of pericarditis (often with effusion) that occurs as a late complication of MI, likely autoimmune in nature.

31. NCLEX Style: The nurse is teaching a patient about lifestyle modifications to reduce the risk of another MI. Which statement by the patient indicates a need for further teaching?

Rationale: Smoking cessation is crucial; merely cutting down is insufficient. Smoking increases heart rate, BP, and CO levels. The other statements are correct.

32. Which of the following conditions is an indication for starting ACE inhibitors within the first 24 hours of MI?

Rationale: ACE inhibitors should be administered within 24 hours to patients with pulmonary congestion or an LV ejection fraction of 40% or lower to prevent remodeling.

33. How does Calcium Channel Blocker therapy benefit patients with Unstable Angina/NSTEMI?

Rationale: Calcium channel blockers (like Verapamil and Diltiazem) decrease myocardial oxygen demand by decreasing afterload, contractility, and heart rate.

34. A patient with chest pain has ST elevation >1 mm in leads II, III, and aVF. Which wall of the heart is likely affected?

Rationale: Though not explicitly detailed in the text, leads II, III, and aVF are standard limb leads associated with the inferior wall. The text mentions ST elevation in contiguous limb leads.

35. NCLEX Style: A patient recovering from a PTCA (angioplasty) complains of wetness at the groin site. The nurse finds active bleeding. What is the PRIORITY action?

Rationale: Complications of PTCA include hemorrhage. The priority is to stop bleeding by applying manual pressure, then assess and notify the provider.

36. Clopidogrel (Plavix) is primarily used in ACS management when:

Rationale: Clopidogrel is an antiplatelet agent used if the patient is intolerant of aspirin or in combination with aspirin for stent placement.

37. Which of the following is a classic characteristic of Unstable Angina pain?

Rationale: Unstable angina often occurs at rest, is more severe/prolonged (>20 mins), and does not follow the predictable pattern of stable angina.

38. A patient is being discharged after an MI. Which statement regarding sexual activity is generally appropriate?

Rationale: General guidelines suggest sexual activity can be resumed when the patient can tolerate mild physical activity (like climbing two flights of stairs) without angina. Prophylactic NTG is not standard advice for all, and beta-blockers don't forbid sex (though may cause ED).

39. The nurse auscultates a new murmur in a patient 3 days post-MI. This could indicate which complication?

Rationale: Transient systolic murmurs may be heard because of papillary muscle dysfunction or rupture, leading to mitral valve regurgitation.

40. NCLEX Style: A patient with acute chest pain asks why they are receiving oxygen even though they are not short of breath. The best response is:

Rationale: The goal of therapy is to restore the balance between oxygen supply and demand. Supplemental oxygen increases the supply to the ischemic myocardium.
