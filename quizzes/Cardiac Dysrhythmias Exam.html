1. A patient's ECG shows ST-segment depression and T-wave inversion. What clinical condition does this primarily suggest?

Rationale: The lecture material specifically states that ischemia is seen on the ECG as ST-segment depressions and T-wave inversions.

2. Which dysrhythmia is characterized by a heart rate that increases with inspiration and decreases with expiration?

Rationale: Sinus arrhythmia is caused by an irregularity in sinus node discharge associated with respiratory phases: rate increases with inspiration and decreases with expiration.

3. A symptomatic patient with Sinus Bradycardia (HR 45 bpm) is being treated. What is the first-line drug of choice mentioned in the text?

Rationale: For symptomatic Sinus Bradycardia, appropriate measures include the administration of Atropine 0.5 to 1 mg.

4. Which of the following is NOT a common cause of Sinus Tachycardia?

Rationale: Fever, anemia, and hyperthyroidism are listed as causes of Sinus Tachycardia. Vagal stimulation (parasympathetic response) slows the heart rate and causes bradycardia, not tachycardia.

5. In Sinus Arrest, the rhythm is irregular because:

Rationale: Sinus arrest occurs when the sinus node fails to form a discharge, producing pauses of varying lengths due to the absence of atrial depolarization.

6. A nurse identifies a Premature Atrial Contraction (PAC) on the monitor. Which feature is characteristic of a PAC?

Rationale: The text describes PACs as having a premature and abnormal or hidden P wave, while the QRS is typically normal (<0.12s). Wide/bizarre QRS refers to ventricular ectopic beats.

7. A patient presents with Paroxysmal Supraventricular Tachycardia (PSVT) at a rate of 180 bpm. Vagal stimulation has failed. What is the next anticipated medication?

Rationale: If vagal stimulation is unsuccessful in terminating PSVT, the text states that IV adenosine may be given.

8. Which ECG characteristic is definitive for Atrial Flutter?

Rationale: Atrial flutter is characterized by a rapid and regular atrial rate (250-350 bpm) that produces "sawtooth" P waves on the ECG.

9. What is a major complication associated with Atrial Fibrillation that requires anticoagulation therapy?

Rationale: The text warns that patients with AF are at risk for the formation of mural thrombi in the fibrillating atrium and embolic events such as stroke, MI, or pulmonary embolus.

10. True or False: Atrial Fibrillation is characterized by a regular ventricular rhythm.

Rationale: False. The text states that in Atrial Fibrillation, the ventricular rhythm is "characteristically irregular."

11. A Premature Ventricular Contraction (PVC) is considered malignant when it occurs more frequently than:

Rationale: The text explicitly states that a PVC is considered malignant when it occurs more than 10 times/hour.

12. Which electrolyte imbalance is listed as a potential cause for myocardial irritability and PVCs?

Rationale: Possible causes for PVCs include myocardial irritability, for example, as a result of hypokalemia.

13. Ventricular Tachycardia (V-Tach) is defined as:

Rationale: The text defines Ventricular Tachycardia as three or more PVCs occurring in a row with rapid ventricular rhythm originating from an ectopic focus.

14. What is the immediate treatment for Pulseless Ventricular Tachycardia?

Rationale: For pulseless V-tachycardia, the text indicates the immediate start of CPR and Defibrillation.

15. A patient is in Ventricular Fibrillation (V-Fib). What does the ECG show?

Rationale: V-Fib is characterized by a chaotic pattern of electrical activities, irregular rhythm, and P/QRS/T waves that cannot be identified.

16. Which of the following is the correct treatment approach for Asystole?

Rationale: Treatment for asystole includes immediate CPR and repeated doses of Epinephrine. Defibrillation is NOT indicated because there is no electrical activity to reorganize.

17. In First-Degree Atrioventricular (AV) Block, the PR interval is:

Rationale: First-degree AV block is characterized by all atrial impulses being conducted to the ventricles but with a PR interval greater than 0.20 seconds.

18. Which AV block is characterized by a progressive lengthening of the PR interval until a P wave is not conducted (dropped beat)?

Rationale: Mobitz Type I (Wenckebach) is defined by AV conduction times that progressively lengthen until a P wave is not conducted.

19. In Third-Degree (Complete) AV Block, what is the relationship between P waves and QRS complexes?

Rationale: In 3rd-degree block, P waves and QRS complexes occur at rates and timing totally independent of each other because no atrial impulses conduct to the ventricles.

20. What is the definitive treatment for a patient with permanent Third-Degree AV Block?

Rationale: The text indicates preparing for implantation of a permanent pacemaker for permanent block to restore adequate cardiac output.

21. Which maneuver is suggested to terminate PSVT by stimulating the vagus nerve?

Rationale: Vagal stimulation, such as Carotid massage, Valsalva maneuver, or immersing the face in cold water, often terminates PSVT.

22. What is the usual rate range for Sinus Bradycardia?

Rationale: In sinus bradycardia, the sinus node initiates impulses at a rate of less than 60 beats/min.

23. Which medication is indicated for unstable Ventricular Tachycardia with a pulse if the condition allows for sedation and cardioversion?

Rationale: The text lists Amiodarone (Cordarone), Procainamide, or Magnesium Sulfate as pharmacological treatments for V-tachycardia with a pulse.

24. Mobitz Type II Second-Degree AV Block is dangerous because:

Rationale: Mobitz II is located below the AV node (bundle of His/Purkinje), features a constant PR interval with sudden dropped QRS complexes, and is more serious than Mobitz I.

25. True or False: Defibrillation is the primary treatment for Asystole.

Rationale: False. Asystole represents a complete cessation of electrical activity. Defibrillation is not effective; CPR and Epinephrine are used.

26. Which of the following is an indication for a temporary or permanent pacemaker in Sinus Bradycardia?

Rationale: Treatment such as pacemaker insertion is indicated if the pulse is very slow and the patient is symptomatic (hemodynamically compromised).

27. Which condition is marked by chaotic atrial activity with a rate of 350-500 bpm and "quivering" waves?

Rationale: Atrial fibrillation is defined as a rapid atrial ectopic rhythm (350-500 bpm) characterized by chaotic atrial activity and small, quivering fibrillatory waves.

28. A patient has a magnesium deficiency. Which dysrhythmia are they most at risk for?

Rationale: Magnesium Sulfate is listed as a treatment for V-Tach, and electrolyte imbalances (including hypokalemia/magnesium) can cause myocardial irritability leading to PVCs and V-Tach.

29. The QRS complex in a Premature Ventricular Contraction (PVC) is typically:

Rationale: PVCs are characterized by a wide and bizarre QRS complex, and the P wave is usually absent or obscured.

30. Which of the following is NOT a sign or symptom of dysrhythmias mentioned in the text?

Rationale: Symptoms listed include palpitations, syncope, chest pain, lightheadedness, SOB, and fatigue. Hyperactivity is not listed.

31. What is the ECG appearance of a "Couplet"?

Rationale: A couplet is defined in the PVC section as "two PVCs in a row".

32. Which of the following indicates "Ventricular Bigeminy"?

Rationale: Ventricular bigeminy is described as a rhythm where "Every other beat is a PVC."

33. PSVT typically starts and ends:

Rationale: The text states that Paroxysmal Supraventricular Tachycardia (PSVT) begins abruptly, usually with a PAC, and ends abruptly.

34. Digoxin toxicity is listed as a possible cause for which dysrhythmia?

Rationale: Digoxin toxicity is explicitly mentioned as a possible cause for Paroxysmal Supraventricular Tachycardia (PSVT).

35. Which drug is mentioned to increase vagal tone, potentially causing Sinus Arrhythmia?

Rationale: The text notes that Sinus Arrhythmia also occurs after enhancement of vagal tone, for example with Digoxin or Morphine.

36. Identify the dysrhythmia: "Sinus node initiates an impulse at a rate of > 100 beats/min."

Rationale: This is the direct definition provided for Sinus Tachycardia.

37. True or False: 12-lead ECG is useful for detecting serum electrolyte imbalances.

Rationale: True. The text states the ECG is useful for detecting serum electrolyte imbalances because of their effects on the electrical impulse of the heart.

38. In Atrial Flutter, the AV node often functions as a "gatekeeper," creating a block (e.g., 2:1, 3:1). What is the purpose of this block?

Rationale: The text explains that the AV node functions as a "gatekeeper" allowing only every 2nd, 3rd, or 4th stimulus to proceed, preventing 1:1 conduction of the rapid flutter rate.

39. When treating Atrial Fibrillation, Cordarone (Amiodarone) is used as an:

Rationale: The text categorizes Cordarone as an Antiarrhythmic medication in the treatment section for Atrial Fibrillation.

40. Which of the following is defined as "variation in the normal pattern of electrical stimulation of the heart"?

Rationale: This is the verbatim definition of Cardiac Dysrhythmia provided at the beginning of the lecture.
