1. According to the text, which demographic is most commonly associated with traumatic brain injuries caused by motor vehicle accidents?

Rationale: The text explicitly states that motor vehicle accidents are a common cause of traumatic brain injury in teenagers & young adults, whereas falling from heights is more common in geriatric & pediatric patients.

2. Which type of skull fracture is described as having no bony displacement and appearing as thin lines radiographically?

Rationale: Linear skull fractures appear as thin lines radiographically and have no bony displacement. Most linear fractures require no treatment.

3. A patient presents with "raccoon eyes" and "Battle's sign" following head trauma. Which type of injury does this suggest?

Rationale: The text lists raccoon eyes, Battle's sign, rhinorrhea, and otorrhea as clinical signs specific to a Basal skull fracture.

4. What is the prophylactic treatment priority for a patient diagnosed with a basilar skull fracture?

Rationale: A concern with basilar skull fractures is infection; therefore, the text states a broad-spectrum antibiotic should be given, and a tetanus injection should be administered prophylactically.

5. An Epidural Hematoma (EDH) is best defined as bleeding occurring in which location?

Rationale: EDH is defined as bleeding between the skull & dura mater. Subdural hematoma occurs between the dura mater and the brain surface.

6. Which of the following best characterizes a concussion?

Rationale: A concussion is a diffuse brain injury resulting from blunt trauma. It is non-lethal and results in immediate & transient loss of consciousness, which is reversible in minutes to hours.

7. The nurse observes "Cushing's Triad" in a patient with a head injury. Which set of vital signs reflects this condition?

Rationale: Cushing's triad occurs in response to increased intracranial pressure (ICP) and consists of the combination of hypertension (increased arterial systolic pressure), bradycardia, and bradypnea.

8. A patient has a Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) score of 10. How is this head injury classified?

Rationale: A GCS score of 9-12 is classified as a Moderate head injury. 13-15 is mild, and 8 or below is severe.

9. True or False: Any patient not obeying commands is considered to have a severe head injury, even if their GCS is slightly higher than 8.

Rationale: The text explicitly states: "Any patient not obeying commands is considered to have a severe head injury even if the Glasgow Coma Score may be slightly higher than 8."

10. In a patient with head trauma, what is the primary cause of hypotension?

Rationale: The text states: "The primary cause of hypotension is hypovolemia due to multisystem trauma."

11. A nurse is positioning an unconscious patient with a head injury. What is the recommended position?

Rationale: The text specifies: "Position the patient in the left lateral position if unconscious and in an upright position if conscious."

12. What is the mechanism of action of Mannitol when used in head injury management?

Rationale: Mannitol and furosemide are used to "promote osmotic diuresis" to reduce cerebral edema.

13. Why is hyperventilation utilized in the management of increased Intracranial Pressure (ICP)?

Rationale: Hyperventilation is used to "decrease PaCO2, which causes cerebral vasoconstriction & leads to a decrease in intracranial pressure." Increased PaCO2 acts as a vasodilator.

14. Which medication is specifically indicated to decrease the metabolic demand of the brain?

Rationale: The text lists "Barbiturate therapy to decrease the metabolic demand of the brain & decreases ICP."

15. A nurse is caring for a patient with increased ICP. Which intervention is appropriate to facilitate venous return?

Rationale: Proper positioning to facilitate venous return involves: neutral head alignment & minimal hip flexion. The text also mentions head elevation.

16. The nurse is monitoring a patient receiving Mannitol (1 g/kg IV stat). Which assessment is critical to prevent complications?

Rationale: The text states to "Reduce cerebral edema through monitoring urinary output through an indwelling catheter system" when administering Mannitol to prevent bladder overdistention & follow the diuretic response.

17. Which analgesic is specifically suggested by the text for pain relief in head-injured patients?

Rationale: Under pain relief, the text recommends: "Use light sedation and analgesia (e.g., codeine phosphate 30-60 mg IM q4h)."

18. Why are stool softeners administered to patients with head injuries?

Rationale: The text states: "Initiate measures to prevent the Valsalva Maneuver (stool softeners)." The Valsalva maneuver increases ICP.

19. Which of the following is considered a "secondary injury" to the brain?

Rationale: Primary injuries (fractures, shearing) occur at the time of trauma. Secondary injuries are the physiologic response and include hypotension, hypoxia, hypercarbia, and increased ICP.

20. What is the goal of administering Anticonvulsants in head injury management?

Rationale: Anticonvulsants are used for early seizure activity because "seizures increase metabolic activity in the brain and lead to an increase in ICP."

21. A "Depressed skull fracture" may specifically require which intervention if bone fragments are lodged in brain tissue?

Rationale: Depressed skull fractures involves inward displacement and "May require surgical treatment (surgical elevation when the depressed bone fragment becomes lodged in brain tissue)."

22. What specific imaging modality is recommended to demonstrate lesions that might not be detected by a CT scan?

Rationale: The text notes: "Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) may demonstrate the existence of lesions that could not be detected by CT."

23. In assessing a patient with high intracranial pressure (ICP), what pupillary response is commonly observed?

Rationale: The text states: "In patients with high intracranial pressure, one or both pupils may be very wide in response to bright color."

24. What is the nutritional protocol for a patient in the acute phase of a head injury as described in the text?

Rationale: The text instructions for Nutrition include: "A nasogastric tube should be inserted" and "Patients should be given nothing by mouth."

25. True or False: Steroids are used in head injury management to prevent cerebral edema.

Rationale: The text lists "Steroids to prevent cerebral edema" as part of the pharmacologic therapy.
