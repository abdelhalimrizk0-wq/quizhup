1. Which of the following best defines Pulmonary Edema?

Rationale: According to the text, Pulmonary Edema is defined as a condition resulting from excessive accumulation of extravascular fluid in the pulmonary interstitial and/or alveolar spaces, altering gas exchange.

2. A patient presents with "pink frothy sputum." This is considered a classic sign of:

Rationale: The text explicitly states that a classic sign of pulmonary edema is the production of pink frothy sputum.

3. Cardiogenic pulmonary edema occurs when which part of the heart fails to pump blood effectively?

Rationale: Cardiogenic pulmonary edema occurs when the left ventricle is not able to pump out the blood it receives from the lungs, increasing pressure in the pulmonary veins and capillaries.

4. Which of the following is a cause of Non-Cardiogenic Pulmonary Edema?

Rationale: Non-cardiogenic pulmonary edema is caused by direct damage to lung tissue or increased capillary permeability. The text lists smoke inhalation, lung infections, toxins, and ARDS as causes.

5. What is the immediate positioning intervention for a patient with Pulmonary Edema?

Rationale: The text states the patient must immediately be placed in an upright position to facilitate breathing.

6. Why is Morphine Sulfate administered to a patient with Pulmonary Edema?

Rationale: Morphine Sulfate is given to decrease anxiety and the work load of breathing.

7. Which medication is used to improve ventricular performance by decreasing preload and peripheral vascular resistance?

Rationale: Vasodilators like nitroglycerin are used to improve ventricular performance by decreasing preload and peripheral vascular resistance.

8. Virchow's Triad, which leads to venous thrombosis, includes all of the following EXCEPT:

Rationale: Virchow's Triad consists of Venous stasis, Injury to the intima, and Changes in coagulation properties (Hypercoagulability). Decreased platelet count is not part of the triad that causes thrombosis.

9. Where do most blood clots that cause Pulmonary Embolism originate?

Rationale: A pulmonary embolism is most often caused by blood clots from veins in the legs (Deep Vein Thrombosis DVT) or in the pelvis/hip area.

10. True or False: Heparin dissolves existing clots.

Rationale: False. The text states: "Heparin does not dissolve an existing clot, but it prevents clot propagation and embolization."

11. A patient with a massive pulmonary embolism is hemodynamically unstable. Which therapy is indicated?

Rationale: Thrombolytic therapy should be considered for patients who are hemodynamically unstable to dissolve the clot.

12. Which blood test is used to rule out the presence of a clot (DVT/PE)?

Rationale: The D-dimer test measures a substance released when a clot breaks up. High levels suggest a clot, while normal levels help rule it out.

13. In the early stage of Pulmonary Embolism, what does the ABG typically reveal?

Rationale: ABGs typically reveal hypoxemia, hypocapnia, and respiratory alkalosis initially due to hyperventilation (tachypnea).

14. What is the purpose of an Inferior Vena Cava (IVC) filter?

Rationale: IVC filters are indicated to prevent further PE in patients with acute venous thromboembolism who have an absolute contraindication to anticoagulant therapy (e.g., recent surgery, bleeding).

15. Which of the following is a symptom of gradual fluid overload in pulmonary edema?

Rationale: Symptoms of gradual fluid overload include Nocturia, Ankle edema, Orthopnea, and Paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea (PND).

16. What does "Orthopnea" mean?

Rationale: The text defines Orthopnea as the inability to lie down flat due to breathlessness.

17. Which medication is an inotropic drug used to strengthen cardiac contractility?

Rationale: Digoxin is listed as an inotropic drug given to strengthen cardiac contractility.

18. A patient on Warfarin should be educated about diet. Which food is high in Vitamin K and should be consumed consistently?

Rationale: The text warns: "Do not change a diet of foods high in Vitamin K (e.g., dark-green vegetables, cauliflower, cabbage...)" as they interact with Warfarin.

19. Which fracture is associated with the highest risk of Venous Thromboembolism?

Rationale: Fractures of the femur and tibia are associated with the highest risk, followed by pelvic and spinal fractures.

20. Aminophylline is used in Pulmonary Edema to:

Rationale: Aminophylline may be given to relax the bronchospasm caused by irritation of pulmonary edema.

21. Approximately what percentage of patients with Pulmonary Embolism die within the first hour?

Rationale: The text states: "Approximately 10% of patients who develop PE die within the first hour."

22. Which of the following is a contraindication for Thrombolytic therapy?

Rationale: Contraindications include recent surgery, recent organ biopsy, pregnancy, recent stroke, or active internal bleeding.

23. Nursing care for a patient with PE includes "Respiratory toilet." What does this include?

Rationale: Respiratory toilet includes nebulizer treatments, incentive spirometry, and postural drainage (vibration and percussion).

24. What is the antidote for Heparin?

Rationale: The text mentions: "N.B anticoagulant antagonists (protamine sulphate) should be readily available."

25. True or False: A patient on Warfarin should be instructed to use a blade razor for shaving.

Rationale: False. The text instructs: "Do not use a blade razor (electric razors preferred)" to reduce the risk of bleeding.

26. When is IVC filter indicated?

Rationale: Indications include patients with acute venous thromboembolism who have an absolute contraindication to anticoagulant therapy or recurrent Pulmonary Embolism.

27. Which of the following drugs is a diuretic used in Pulmonary Edema management?

Rationale: Furosemide (Lasix) is the diuretic mentioned to reduce intravascular fluid volume.

28. Which condition involves "excessive accumulation of extravascular fluid in the pulmonary interstitial and/or alveolar spaces"?

Rationale: This is the direct definition of Pulmonary Edema provided in the text.

29. High Altitude Pulmonary Edema (HAPE) is a type of:

Rationale: HAPE is listed under Non-cardiogenic pulmonary edema causes in the text.

30. A patient reports sudden shortness of breath and a sense of "impending doom." You suspect:

Rationale: Clinical manifestations of PE include sudden cough, dyspnea, and the patient being "extremely apprehensive with a sense of impending doom."

31. Which of the following is a risk factor for PE?

Rationale: Obesity and Pregnancy are explicitly listed as risk factors for PE.

32. How does PEEP (Positive End-Expiratory Pressure) help in Pulmonary Edema?

Rationale: PEEP is applied to prevent closure of small airways and alveoli.

33. If a patient is HIPA (Heparin-Induced Platelet Aggregation) positive, what is the priority nursing action?

Rationale: If HIPA positive, instruct about the importance of avoiding heparin; continuation is contraindicated.

34. Which diagnostic test allows visualization of a weak heart muscle or leaky valves?

Rationale: An Echocardiogram is used to see if there is a weak heart muscle, leaky or narrow heart valves, or fluid surrounding the heart.

35. What is the main goal of management for Pulmonary Edema?

Rationale: The main goals include all three: Improve gas exchange, reduce extravascular fluid, and improve myocardial function.

36. Signs of excessive anticoagulation include all EXCEPT:

Rationale: Signs of excessive anticoagulation are related to bleeding (nose bleeds, black stools, blood in urine). Hypertension is not a sign of bleeding/excess anticoagulation (hypotension would be).

37. During the transition from Heparin to Warfarin, what is required?

Rationale: The onset of Warfarin is 2-3 days. There needs to be an overlap of these medications to ensure adequate PT/INR levels before discontinuing Heparin.

38. Nursing monitoring for Pulmonary Edema includes checking sputum. What characteristics are you looking for?

Rationale: "Note color, consistency and odor of sputum (pink frothy is usually noted in Pulmonary Edema)."

39. A patient with PE is ordered "strict bed rest" initially. What is the rationale?

Rationale: Bed rest decreases the oxygen demand during an episode of acute respiratory distress.

40. Which vital sign changes would you expect in early hypoxia/hypercapnia?

Rationale: In initial hypoxia and hypercapnia, there is an increase in respiratory rate, heart rate, and blood pressure.

41. Why should IV catheters not be left in place for long periods in patients at risk for PE?

Rationale: "Avoid leaving IV catheters in place for long periods of time" is listed under Prevention to avoid venous stasis and potential thrombus sites.

42. Dobutamine is used in Pulmonary Edema to:

Rationale: Dobutamine dilates the peripheral vessels to decrease the work load of the left ventricle.

43. What is Embolectomy?

Rationale: Embolectomy is clot removal by surgical means or via a catheter.

44. Pulmonary Edema causes a ventilation-perfusion mismatch leading to:

Rationale: Pulmonary edema can cause ventilation-perfusion mismatch, increases the shunting of non-oxygenated blood leading to hypoxemia.

45. Which of the following is NOT a symptom of Pulmonary Embolism?

Rationale: Tachycardia (rapid heart rate), not Bradycardia (slow heart rate), is a symptom of PE.

46. What type of shock can result from a massive Pulmonary Embolism?

Rationale: PE increases right ventricular afterload, leading to RV failure and Hemodynamic collapse.

47. Estrogen-containing birth control pills:

Rationale: Estrogen-containing birth control pills have increased the occurrence of venous thromboembolism in healthy women.

48. When assessing for calf tenderness, redness, and swelling, you are screening for:

Rationale: Calf tenderness, redness, and swelling are signs of DVT, which PE often arises from.

49. What precaution should be taken with IM injections in a patient receiving thrombolytic therapy?

Rationale: Measures to reduce the risk of bleeding include "avoiding intramuscular (IM) injections."

50. A patient has cool, pale skin and bluish nails. This is a compensatory response to:

Rationale: "Cool, pale skin occurs as a compensatory response to hypoxemia. When oxygen and perfusion become impaired peripheral tissues become cyanotic."
