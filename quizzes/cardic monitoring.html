1. What is the primary purpose of cardiac monitoring?

Rationale: Cardiac monitoring refers to the continuous or intermittent monitoring of heart activity by electrocardiography, with assessment of the patient's condition relative to their cardiac rhythm.

2. All of the following are purposes of a cardiac monitor EXCEPT:

Rationale: Cardiac monitors are diagnostic and monitoring tools. They display ECG, pressures, and O2 saturation, but they do not administer medications.

3. Which type of monitoring system requires the patient to be linked directly to the monitor via an ECG cable and is common in ICUs?

Rationale: Hard-wire monitors, commonly used in ICU settings, require the patient to be linked directly to the cardiac monitor through the ECG cable, which limits patient mobility.

4. What is the MAIN advantage of a telemetry monitoring system?

Rationale: Telemetry systems use a battery-operated transmitter and radiofrequency signals, so no direct wire is needed. This is used in areas where the patient is fairly mobile.

5. True or False: In telemetry monitoring, stable ECG tracings are often *more* difficult to obtain because the patient is mobile.

Rationale: True. The document states, "Because the patient is mobile, stable ECG tracings often are more difficult to obtain" with telemetry systems.

6. A standard 12-lead ECG uses how many lead wires and electrodes?

Rationale: A standard ECG uses 10 lead wires with electrodes at the ends (4 placed on the limbs, and 6 placed on the chest) to produce 12 electrical views (leads).

7. What is a key difference between a 3-electrode and a 5-electrode monitoring system?

Rationale: A 3-electrode system produces a limited selection (Leads I, II, or III). A 5-electrode system adds a chest electrode, allowing viewing of any of the 12 ECG leads.

8. Which monitoring lead is commonly used because it records clear, upright P waves and QRS complexes?

Rationale: Lead II is used commonly because it records clear upright P waves and QRS complexes, which are helpful in determining the underlying rhythm.

9. A nurse wants to monitor a patient for ischemia in the anterior wall of the heart. Which leads would be most helpful?

Rationale: Leads V1 through V4 are helpful in detecting ischemia, injury, and infarction in the anterior wall.

10. Leads II, III, and aVF are most useful for detecting ischemia in which wall of the heart?

Rationale: Leads II, III, and aVF are helpful in detecting ischemia, injury, and infarction in the inferior wall.

11. Which leads are helpful for detecting ischemia in the lateral wall?

Rationale: Leads I, aVL, V5, and V6 are helpful in detecting ischemia, injury, and infarction in the lateral wall.

12. Leads V1 (or MCL1) are particularly useful for differentiating ventricular ectopy from what?

Rationale: Lead V1 (or MCL1) is helpful to differentiate ventricular ectopy from supraventricular rhythm aberrantly conducted in the ventricles.

13. What is the FIRST step in the assessment phase of the cardiac monitoring procedure?

Rationale: The procedure lists assessing pulse, vital signs, heart/lung sounds, LOC, etc., as the first step "to provide baseline assessment data."

14. Why is it important to explain the monitoring procedure to the patient and family?

Rationale: The rationale for explaining the procedure is "To decrease fear and anxiety."

15. What position should the patient be assisted into for electrode placement?

Rationale: The nurse should "Assist the patient in supine position" to "enable easy access to the chest for electrodes placement."

16. True or False: Electrodes should be connected to the lead wires *after* placing them on the patient's skin to ensure a good connection.

Rationale: False. The procedure is to "Connect the electrodes to the lead wires *before* placing the electrodes on the patient" to "prevent discomfort and development of air bubbles in the electrode gel."

17. In a 3-lead system, which electrode is placed on the left lower rib cage (8th ICS, MCL)?

Rationale: For a 3-lead system, the LL (red) electrode is placed on the left lower rib cage (8th ICS, MCL).

18. In a 5-lead system, the RA (white) electrode is placed where?

Rationale: In a 5-lead system, the RA (white) electrode is placed "below right clavicle, 2nd ICS, MCL."

19. In a 5-lead system, which electrode is placed on the right lower rib cage (8th ICS, MCL)?

Rationale: The RL (green) electrode is placed on the "right lower rib cage, 8th ICS, MCL."

20. What is the color of the LA electrode in *both* a 3-lead and 5-lead system?

Rationale: The LA (black) electrode is placed "below left clavicle, 2nd ICS, MCL" in both 3-lead and 5-lead systems.

21. In a 5-lead system, the "Chest" electrode is what color and is usually placed at which V lead position?

Rationale: The 5th electrode is the Chest (brown) lead, which can be placed at "any V lead, usually V1."

22. What is the purpose of setting alarms on the cardiac monitor?

Rationale: The rationale for setting alarms is "To activate monitor alarm system," with limits set based on the "patient's current clinical status and heart rate."

23. All of the following should be documented after the procedure EXCEPT:

Rationale: Documentation should include objective data like the date/time, lead system, and vital signs. Personal opinions are not part of professional documentation.

24. Which of the following is an "unexpected outcome" that should be reported?

Rationale: The document lists "Altered skin integrity," "False alarm," "Abnormal ECG," and "Hemodynamic instability" as unexpected outcomes to be reported.

25. True or False: It is safe to use ungrounded electrical equipment as long as the patient is dry.

Rationale: False. A special consideration is to "Make sure all electrical equipment and outlets are grounded to avoid electric shock and interference."

26. Why should electrode packages NOT be opened until just before use?

Rationale: Electrode packages should be kept sealed "to prevent the gel from drying out," as dry gel leads to poor signal conduction.

27. Electrodes should NOT be placed on which of the following areas?

Rationale: It is important to "Avoid placing the electrodes on bony prominences, hairy locations, areas where defibrillator pads will be placed, or areas for chest compression."

28. What should be done if the patient's skin is very oily or diaphoretic?

Rationale: "If the patient skin is very oily or diaphoretic, rub the electrode site with a dry gauze pad before applying the electrode to help reduce interference."

29. The three basic components of a monitoring system include electrodes, a monitoring cable, and a:

Rationale: The three basic components are: 1. Electrodes, 2. The monitoring cable, and 3. The display screen.

30. Indications for cardiac monitoring include all of the following EXCEPT:

Rationale: Monitoring is indicated for "All patients in critical care units" and "patients in selected acute care settings, post-anesthesia areas, operating rooms & emergency departments," not necessarily all stable patients on a general floor.

31. True or False: A 5-electrode system can only view one lead at a time (single-channel recording).

Rationale: False. A 3-electrode system is limited to a single-channel recording. A 5-electrode system permits the nurse to "view two or more leads on the monitor screen simultaneously (multichannel recording)."

32. The normal route of depolarization moves from the SA node, downward through the AV node, and to the left because:

Rationale: Depolarization moves "downward" and "to the left because the muscle mass in the left side of the heart is greater than the muscle mass of the right side."

33. Why is it important for the patient to report symptoms like pain, palpitation, or chest discomfort?

Rationale: The rationale is "To ensure appropriate and timely assessment and intervention" when symptoms occur, as they may correlate with ECG changes.

34. A "hard-wire" monitor operates on electricity but is isolated so that fluids do NOT pose an electrical hazard, as long as:

Rationale: The document states that hard-wire monitors are well isolated "as long as the machine is maintained properly."

35. Which action helps reduce the transmission of microorganisms after the procedure?

Rationale: In the post-care (Nurse) section, the rationale for removing gloves and hand washing is "To reduce the transmission of microorganism."
