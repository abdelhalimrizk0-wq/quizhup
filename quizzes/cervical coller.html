1. What is the definition of a cervical collar?

Rationale: A cervical collar, also known as a neck brace, is a medical device used to support a person's neck. It can help realign the spinal cord and relieve pain.

2. What is a primary purpose of a cervical collar?

Rationale: One of the main purposes is to "Immobilize the c-spine to limit motion" and "prevent further injury."

3. A cervical collar is designed to restrict which of the following motions?

Rationale: The purpose is to "Immobilize the c-spine to limit motion. Restricts neck flexion, extension, and lateral tilt (bending & rotation)."

4. While a cervical collar restricts most neck movements, which motion does it NOT restrict?

Rationale: The document states that a collar "Restricts neck flexion, extension, and lateral tilt... however, doesn't restrict axial loading."

5. Which of the following is an example of a "non-acute injury" indication for a cervical collar?

Rationale: The document lists "strained cervical injuries" as an example of a non-acute injury. Arthritis and metastasis are listed as chronic conditions.

6. Arthritis or cervical metastasis are examples of what kind of indication for a cervical collar?

Rationale: The document lists "arthritis or cervical metastasis" as examples of "Chronic conditions."

7. In an acute injury, a cervical collar is primarily used for:

Rationale: An indication is the "Prevention of cervical spine fracture or spinal cord injury after an acute injury."

8. Which type of collar is flexible, made of polyurethane foam, and secured with Velcro?

Rationale: "Soft collars are flexible and made of polyurethane foam rubber. These collars are easily wrapped around the neck and secured with Velcro."

9. What is the MAIN purpose of a soft collar?

Rationale: Soft collars are usually worn after a more rigid collar. "The main purpose of a soft collar is to help you gradually transition to not wearing a brace."

10. True or False: Hard collars are made of a soft polyurethane foam material.

Rationale: False. "Hard collars look similar to soft collars, but they are made of a hard polyethylene material."

11. Which collar is described as a stiff foam collar made of 2 pieces attached with Velcro, supporting the lower jaw and upper thoracic spine?

Rationale: "A Philadelphia collar is a stiff foam collar composed of 2 pieces that are attached on the sides with Velcro. The upper portion... supports the lower jaw and the brace extends down to cover the upper thoracic spine."

12. Why is a Philadelphia collar often prescribed after cervical spine surgery?

Rationale: "Because it's so effective at limiting movement, the Philadelphia collar is often prescribed after cervical spine surgery to promote a healthy recovery."

13. True or False: An individual's height has a direct relationship to the length of their neck, making sizing easy.

Rationale: False. The document states, "It is crucial to note that the height of an individual has no relationship to the length of his or her neck."

14. What is the common method described for measuring a cervical collar size?

Rationale: The method described involves resting an outstretched hand on the shoulder and counting the number of fingers from the pinky to an imaginary line extending from the patient's chin.

15. When fitting a cervical collar, it should be snug enough to prevent nodding, but NOT so tight that it:

Rationale: "Once secured, the collar should be snug enough to keep your patient from nodding... but not so tight as to keep him from opening his mouth."

16. What is a key tip for wearing a cervical collar?

Rationale: A key tip is to "Focus on good posture. Try not to slouch or hunch over." The document specifically says to AVOID soft, low chairs.

17. Patients wearing a cervical collar should avoid:

Rationale: The tips include "Avoid sitting in soft, low chairs" as this can affect posture and put extra pressure on the neck.

18. What is a major complication of cervical collars related to skin?

Rationale: A listed complication is "Skin Damage, especially at risk for pressure ulcers." Specific areas of concern include the chin, ears, and sternum.

19. Cervical collars can cause limitations in swallowing, coughing, and breathing, which can potentially lead to what complication?

Rationale: The document lists "Swallowing, coughing, breathing, and vomiting limitations, potentially causing aspiration" as a complication.

20. In the procedure for application, what is the role of the FIRST aider (First Aider One)?

Rationale: The first aider (one) is responsible to "Stabilize the head in the neutral position by both hands and spread fingers."

21. What is the role of the SECOND aider (First Aider Two) during application?

Rationale: The second aider is responsible for measuring the neck, selecting/adjusting the collar, and sliding it into place.

22. According to Patient Education, what must a patient NOT do while wearing a collar?

Rationale: The patient education section explicitly states, "Should not drive." It also says to take care *when* walking, not to avoid it entirely.

23. How should a patient move from a lying to a standing position?

Rationale: Patient education includes: "When moving from a lying to a standing position, they must use their arm and leg muscles to keep their spine in proper alignment."

24. For skin care, how often should the skin under the collar be inspected?

Rationale: "Inspect patient's skin under and around the cervical collar at least twice a day."

25. A safety consideration is to remind the patient to inform the nurse if they experience:

Rationale: A key safety consideration is to "Remind the patient to inform you (if able) and/or physician if he/she experiences any sudden neck or back pain, and/or numbness or tingling."
