1. What is the definition of a chest tube drainage system?

Rationale: A chest tube drainage system is "a closed system designed to facilitate the evacuation of fluid, blood, and air from the pleural space... to restore negative pressure... and promote re-expansion of a collapsed lung."

2. What is the normal intrapleural pressure during expiration?

Rationale: "Normal intrapleural pressure measures approximately (-4) cmH2O during expiration. At the end of inspiration, pressure decreases to (-8) cmH2O."

3. What is a typical chest tube size for men?

Rationale: The document lists the size for Men as 28-32F.

4. What is a typical chest tube size for a neonate?

Rationale: The document lists the size for Neonates as 10-12F.

5. Which of the following is a primary purpose of a chest tube drainage system?

Rationale: The purposes include: "Promote re-expansion of the lung," "Reestablish normal negative pressure," and "To remove air and fluid from the pleural space."

6. What is a hemothorax?

Rationale: A hemothorax is a "(collection of blood in the pleural space)."

7. What is a pneumothorax?

Rationale: A pneumothorax is a "(collection of air in the pleural space)."

8. To drain air, where is the chest tube typically inserted?

Rationale: "To drain air: tube can be placed or inserted near the apex of the lung (2nd intercostal space, mid-clavicle line anteriorly)."

9. To drain fluid, where is the chest tube typically inserted?

Rationale: "To drain fluid: tube can be placed near the base of the lung (4th to 5th intercostal spaces, mid-axillary line)."

10. Where are mediastinal tubes typically placed?

Rationale: "Mediastinal tubes are placed during surgery... exiting from the anterior mediastinum below the xiphoid process (sternum)..."

11. Which of the following is listed as a potential complication of chest tube insertion?

Rationale: The document lists improper placement, bleeding, empyema, and organ penetration as complications.

12. What is a key assessment finding for a tension pneumothorax?

Rationale: A sign of tension pneumothorax is "Tracheal deviation to unaffected side." Other signs include neck vein distension and muffled heart sounds.

13. What percussion sound would you expect to find on the affected side of a pneumothorax?

Rationale: "Hyper-resonance in the affected side (pneumothorax)." This is due to the trapped air.

14. What percussion sound would you expect to find on the affected side of a hemothorax?

Rationale: "dullness in the affected side (hemothorax...)." This is due to the collection of fluid (blood).

15. Why is IV access established or confirmed before chest tube insertion?

Rationale: The rationale is "To provide a route for analgesia, sedation, and emergency medication."

16. What is the appropriate patient position for inserting a chest tube for a hemothorax?

Rationale: The document lists "Semi-fowler position (for hemothorax)." This helps fluid settle to the base of the lung. "Lateral, supine" is listed for pneumothorax.

17. What is the rationale for placing split drain sponges and 4x4 gauze over the insertion site?

Rationale: "Split drain sponges are placed around the chest tube... Cover with 4x4 gauze... Tape dressing." The rationale is to "Provide an airtight seal around the chest tube."

18. Why are all connection points on the drainage system taped or secured?

Rationale: Taping connections is done "To create an airtight system; airtight connections prevent air leaks into the pleural space."

19. What is the rationale for placing the patient in a semi-Fowler's position after the tube is inserted?

Rationale: "To facilitate drainage from the lung by allowing air to rise and fluid to settle... To make breathing easier."

20. What is "tidaling" in a chest tube drainage system?

Rationale: Tidaling is the "rise and fall (tidaling) of the column" or "fluctuations or tidaling of fluid level in the water seal chamber" that corresponds with respiration.

21. What does persistent or continuous bubbling in the water seal chamber indicate?

Rationale: "Persistence bubbling indicates an air leak either in the patient's lung or in the chest drainage system." (Intermittent bubbling with coughing is normal).

22. The physician should be notified if drainage is more than:

Rationale: "Notify the physician if the drainage is more than 100 cc per hour." (The "Report" section also mentions >=200ml/hr, but this is a more specific instruction).

23. In a one-bottle system, the long tube should be immersed under sterile water by how much?

Rationale: "...one is long and it should be immersed under the level of normal saline or sterile water by 2 cm..."

24. In a two-bottle system, what is the function of the first bottle?

Rationale: "One bottle serves as the drainage-collection bottle and the second is the water seal drainage."

25. What does the third bottle add to a three-bottle system?

Rationale: "This system consists of the standard two-bottle system with the addition of a suction control bottle."

26. What is the FIRST nursing action if a chest tube is accidentally dislodged?

Rationale: The document lists the steps in order: "Encourage patient to cough forcibly," "Apply occlusive dressing," "Notify physician," and "Observe... status." The very first action listed is to have the patient cough.

27. Clamping a chest tube is NOT recommended, but it may be done in which situation?

Rationale: Clamping is avoided but is permissible in specific situations, such as "To quickly empty or change collection bottle or chamber." It should *not* be clamped during transport.

28. Why is routine chest tube stripping or milking NOT recommended?

Rationale: "Routine chest tube stripping or milking is not recommended" because "To prevent transient high negative pressures in the pleural space that could lead to lung entrapment."

29. What item should always be kept at the bedside of a patient with a chest tube?

Rationale: "Ensure that a bottle of sterile water or saline is at the patient's bedside" to "create a temporary water seal in case the drainage container is broken."

30. What is the rationale for encouraging the patient to perform coughing and breathing exercises?

Rationale: "Encourage patient to do coughing and breathing exercises" to "promote drainage and lung expansion."
