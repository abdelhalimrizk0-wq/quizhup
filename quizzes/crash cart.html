1. What is the definition of a crash cart?

Rationale: A crash cart is defined as "a portable trolley containing all equipment, supplies, and drugs required for cardiopulmonary resuscitation and emergency care."

2. What is one of the primary purposes of a crash cart?

Rationale: A purpose of the crash cart is "To initiate necessary treatment in emergency and life-threatening situations in all health care facilities."

3. A crash cart is indicated for use when a patient's:

Rationale: "This emergency equipment is used only if the patient's heart or lungs stop working. The cart is brought to the patient's bedside when the patient's heart or lungs are failing or have failed."

4. True or False: Using the tools on a crash cart requires certification in Advanced Cardiac Life Support (ACLS).

Rationale: True. The document states, "Using the tools on a crash cart requires advanced medical skills and certification in Advanced Cardiac Life Support (ACLS)."

5. What piece of equipment is typically found on top of the crash cart?

Rationale: The "Crash cart top" section lists the "Portable monitor/defibrillator unit with charged batteries." The cardiac board is on the back and the oxygen cylinder is on the side.

6. Where is the cardiac board located?

Rationale: The document specifies that the "Crash cart back" contains the "Cardiac board."

7. What is located on the side of the crash cart?

Rationale: The "Crash cart sides" section lists the "Oxygen cylinder." The sharps container and suction are listed on the top.

8. Which drawer contains adult medications like Epinephrine, Atropine, and Amiodarone?

Rationale: "Drawer 1: Adult Medications" lists all the primary resuscitation drugs.

9. What is the MAIN category of items found in Drawer 2?

Rationale: The document labels "Drawer 2: Breathing and Airway (large drawer)."

10. All of the following are found in the Airway drawer (Drawer 2) EXCEPT:

Rationale: IV start kits are found in Drawer 3 (Circulation and IV supplies). All the other items are listed in Drawer 2 (Breathing and Airway).

11. Which drawer contains syringes of various sizes (3cc, 5cc, 10cc, etc.), blood tubes, and IV start kits?

Rationale: "Drawer 3: Circulation and IV supplies" contains syringes, blood tubes, and IV start kits.

12. IV solutions such as Normal Saline (500ml) and D5% (250ml) are found in which drawer?

Rationale: "Drawer 4: Circulation and tubing (large drawer)" lists IV fluids like Lactating Ringer, Normal Saline, and D5%.

13. Which drawer contains miscellaneous items like chest tubes, sutures, and a cut down tray?

Rationale: "Drawer 5: Others (large drawer)" contains items such as ECG electrodes, chest tubes, sutures, and a cut down tray.

14. What is the correct organizational order for the top drawers of a crash cart?

Rationale: The (N.B) note states: "Drawers are organized and arranged from top to bottom in the following order: medications, airway, circulation, IV solution and tubing, others."

15. What is a key nursing responsibility at the beginning of a shift?

Rationale: "At the beginning of a nursing shift, the nurse responsible for the crash cart should: 1. Check that the defibrillator is charged. 2. Check the content of the crash cart... compared with the crash cart checklist."

16. What is the FIRST priority for the nurse after a crash cart has been used in an emergency?

Rationale: "After using a crash cart: 1. Content should be replaced immediately after use. After each emergency, the team nurse replaces used emergency supplies."

17. True or False: The nurse who used the medications is responsible for going to the pharmacy to get replacements.

Rationale: False. The procedure is: "If indicated, the team nurse *contacts the pharmacist* for drug replacement." The nurse doesn't necessarily leave the unit to get them.

18. True or False: After use, the cart is cleaned, covered, and left unlocked for the next emergency.

Rationale: False. The procedure is: "The cart is cleaned, covered and *locked*." A security seal is also mentioned as a requirement.

19. How often must the crash cart be checked for the expiry dates of its *supplies*?

Rationale: "The crash cart must be checked *monthly* for the expiry dates of its supplies." (Medications are checked for expiration up to every 6 months).

20. Laryngoscopes should be checked at what interval?

Rationale: "Laryngoscopes will be checked prior to placement on the cart and monthly."

21. At what pressure level should an oxygen cylinder be replaced?
