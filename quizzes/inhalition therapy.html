1. What is the definition of a nebulizer?

Rationale: A nebulizer "is a device used to administer medication in the form of a mist inhaled into the lungs."

2. How do nebulizers work?

Rationale: "Nebulizers convert liquids into aerosols of a size that can be inhaled into the lower respiratory tract."

3. Salbutamol and Terbutaline are examples of which class of nebulizer drugs?

Rationale: The document lists "Beta2 agonists (for example salbutamol, terbutaline)."

4. Ipratropium bromide is an example of which class of nebulizer drugs?

Rationale: The document lists "Anticholinergics (for example ipratropium bromide)."

5. Budesonide and Beclometasone are examples of which class of nebulizer drugs?

Rationale: The document lists "Corticosteroids (for example budesonide, beclometasone)."

6. Which of the following is an indication for nebulizer therapy?

Rationale: The document lists Asthma, Cystic fibrosis, COPD, and Respiratory distress as indications for nebulizer use.

7. All of the following are purposes of a nebulizer EXCEPT:

Rationale: Nebulizers deliver inhaled medications, not intravenous medications. The other options are listed as purposes of bronchial hygiene.

8. What is the advantage of a soft mist inhaler?

Rationale: "The device features no gas propellant and no need for battery/power to operate."

9. What is a disadvantage of the soft mist inhaler?

Rationale: A disadvantage is the average droplet size of 5.8 micrometers, "which could indicate some potential efficiency problems for the inhaled medicine to reach the lung."

10. Which type of nebulizer, also called an "atomizer," uses compressed air or oxygen to turn liquid medicine into an aerosol?

Rationale: "The most commonly used nebulizers are jet nebulizers, which are also called 'atomizers'. Jet nebulizers are connected by tubing to a compressor that causes compressed air or oxygen to flow..."

11. Which type of nebulizer uses a vibrating element and high-frequency vibration to produce a vapor mist?

Rationale: The ultrasonic wave nebulizer "produces a vibrating element in contact with a liquid reservoir and its high-frequency vibration is sufficient to produce a vapor mist."

12. What are the disadvantages of an ultrasonic wave nebulizer?

Rationale: Disadvantages include "Having too much liquid waste" and "Causing undesired heating of the medical liquid."

13. Which type of nebulizer is specifically used to deliver medication to patients on a mechanical ventilator?

Rationale: An "In-line nebulizer... is used to deliver medication to patients who are being mechanically ventilated."

14. What is the primary use of a Large Volume Nebulizer?

Rationale: A large volume nebulizer is "Primarily used to administer solution to hydrate the airway and reduce inflammation."

15. What is the primary use of a Small Volume Nebulizer?

Rationale: "It is primarily used to administer drugs." It is connected to a mouthpiece or mask.

16. Which of the following factors affects the efficiency of drug delivery via nebulizer?

Rationale: The document lists all these and more (type of nebulizer, age, lung condition) as factors affecting efficiency.

17. During the assessment phase, why should the nurse check the patient's last meal time?

18. What assessment should be done before starting the nebulizer therapy?

Rationale: The assessment phase includes: "Auscultation patients' breathing sound and assess breathing pattern" and checking "Heart rate, respiratory rate and SpO2" to get a baseline.

19. What is the recommended patient position for nebulizer therapy?

Rationale: The patient should be put in "semi-fowlers' position" to "improve patient lung expansion."

20. True or False: It is important to check the efficiency of the nebulizer machine or oxygen source *after* adding the medication.

Rationale: False. The machine should be checked "before beginning the procedure" to "detect any malfunctioning."

21. If using an oxygen source, what is the correct oxygen flow rate for a nebulizer?

Rationale: "connect to an oxygen source and adjust oxygen flow to at least 10 L/min but not more than 14 L/min."

22. How should the patient be instructed to breathe during the nebulizer treatment?

Rationale: "Instruct the patient to breathe slowly and deeply until all the medication is nebulized" to "ensure good inhalation of medication."

23. Which of the following is an adverse effect the nurse should observe for during administration?

Rationale: The nurse should "Observe for medication adverse effects... such as tachycardia, dyspnea, flushing."

24. What should the nurse assess AFTER the nebulizer treatment?

Rationale: Post-care includes assessing "patient heart rate... Respiratory rate, breathing pattern, breathing sound and SpO2" to "assess patient response to nebulizer."

25. Why should the nurse encourage the patient to cough after the treatment?

Rationale: "Encourage patient to cough after several deep breaths" to "eliminate secretions from the patient's chest."

26. Why is oral care performed after nebulizer therapy?

Rationale: "Perform oral care as needed" to "clean the mouth from the bad taste of medication." (This is especially important with corticosteroids to prevent thrush).

27. Why is face care performed after a nebulizer mask treatment?

Rationale: "Perform face care as needed" to "avoid irritation of the eye from medication."

Rationale: The document lists Bronchospasm, Dyspnea, and Infection from contaminated equipment as potential complications.
