1. What is the definition of the log roll maneuver?

Rationale: "The log roll or logrolling is a maneuver used to move a patient without flexing the spinal column."

2. What is the MAIN principle of the log rolling procedure?

Rationale: "The main principles... are the strict adherence to correct anatomical alignment in order to prevent the possibility of further, catastrophic neurologic injury..."

3. One purpose of the log roll maneuver is to maintain spinal alignment for patients with:

Rationale: A purpose is "To maintain alignment of the spine while turning and moving the patient who has had spinal surgery or suspected or documented spinal injury."

4. Besides maintaining spinal alignment, log rolling is also used to prevent:

Rationale: Another purpose of the log roll maneuver is "To prevent pressure sores."

5. Which of the following is listed as an indication for a log roll?

Rationale: Indications include "Examination of the patient's back," "Cervical collar care," and "Pressure care."

6. True or False: Log rolling can be used to facilitate medical interventions, such as with a vomiting patient.

Rationale: True. An indication is "To facilitate medical interventions such as with the vomiting patient" (to clear the airway while maintaining spinal alignment).

7. What are the two types of log rolling maneuvers mentioned in the text?

Rationale: The document lists "1. Pre-hospital procedure" and "2. In-hospital procedure" as the types of log rolling.

8. A log roll is BEST performed using how many people?

Rationale: "A log roll is best performed using four to six first aiders..."

9. Who is in control of the log roll?

Rationale: The "Head hold person... is in control of the roll. They must ensure all members of the team are ready before proceeding and should give clear instructions."

10. What is the MAIN responsibility of the head hold person?

Rationale: The head hold person "manages cervical spine alignment and is in control of the roll."

11. Who should ideally be the "chest person" in the team?

Rationale: The "Chest person: Should be the tallest person in the team who places hands over the patient's shoulder and lower back."

12. What is the "hip person" responsible for ensuring?

Rationale: "Hip person: This person is responsible for ensuring the lower spine is not twisted during the roll."

13. A "leg person" is required in all the following situations EXCEPT:

Rationale: A leg person is *not* always required. They *are* required for tall, heavy, or plastered patients.

14. What is the role of the fifth (or extra) staff member during a log roll?

Rationale: A fifth member is needed "to perform any required procedure (assessment of the patient's back) or placing spinal board."

15. Log rolling should NOT be provided if:

Rationale: "Logrolling technique should not be provided when there are not enough personnel to safely perform the technique or when other conditions are a priority; eg. Need to obtain and secure an airway..."

16. What is the rationale for assessing the need for log rolling?

Rationale: The rationale for assessing the need is "To protect patient from complication of cervical spine injury."

17. What must be ensured about the patient BEFORE starting the log roll procedure?

Rationale: Preparation includes: "Ensure that the collar is well fitting prior to commencement" and "Ensure that the patient is anatomically aligned prior to commencement..."

18. Why must the patient be anatomically aligned before the roll?

Rationale: The rationale is "To facilitate log rolling and reduce risk of complication."

19. What is the FIRST action performed by the team leader during the implementation phase?

Rationale: "Head hold person (the leader): manually stabilize the patient's head with both hands."

20. What parts of the patient does the "chest person" and "hip person" grasp?

Rationale: "The chest person reaches across the patient, securely grasping the upper arm." and "Hip person reaches across the injured person and grasps the hip."

21. After the head person counts 1, 2, 3, the coordinated roll is performed:

Rationale: "...a coordinated roll of the patient towards the hip & chest person is performed..."

22. What is done immediately after the patient is rolled?

Rationale: "Slide the board against the patient's back... or perform the necessary procedure needed as back assessment."

23. After the procedure is complete, what is done for the patient?

Rationale: Post-care involves: "Return the patient to supine position... or support the patient in a lateral position with wedge pillows."

24. What information should be recorded in the documentation?

Rationale: Documentation includes: "Record use of logrolling technique and number of staff that participated."
