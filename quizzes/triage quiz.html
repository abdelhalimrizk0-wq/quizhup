Triage Principles Quiz

تم صنع هذا الامتحان بواسطة عبدالحليم رزق

1. According to the introduction, triage is described as a system for what?
Rationale: The text states, "Triage is a system for rationing resources in response to an overwhelming medical emergency event."
2. The text states that triage is a process that needs to be:
Rationale: The text explains, "Triage is a process that needs to be monitored throughout the event; patients' condition may change, or resources may become more or less available... necessitating a re-categorizing of patients."
3. The word "triage" originates from the French verb "trier," which means:
Rationale: As stated in the "History of triage" section, "Triage comes from the French verb, trier, which means 'to sort' or 'to choose.'"
4. What is the "emerging concept in triage" that refers to a health care system's ability to expand quickly?
Rationale: The definition section says, "An emerging concept in triage is surge management-a health care system's ability to expand quickly... to meet an increased demand for medical care..."
5. What is the stated "goal of surge management"?
Rationale: The text explicitly states, "The goal of surge management is 'to maintain operations and increase capacity in order to preserve the life and safety of the patients and ensure appropriate healthcare delivery to the community.'"
6. Which of the following is NOT listed as a strategy for managing *staff* during a surge?
Rationale: "Expansion into approved alternate surge areas" is listed as a strategy for the management of *space*, not staff. The other three options are all listed under the "management of staff."
7. What is described as an "extreme" strategy for the management of *materials*?
Rationale: The text lists several strategies for managing materials, "and, in extreme cases, rationing of supplies or materials."
8. When does Primary Triage occur at the scene of a multiple-casualty incident?
Rationale: Under "Primary Triage," the text states it "Occurs immediately upon the arrival of the first EMS crew."
9. What is the purpose of Secondary Triage?
Rationale: The text states, "Once the patients are moved from the scene to the triage unit, the secondary triage occurs. The secondary triage is designed to reevaluate the patient categorization..."
10. The START system is a mnemonic for:
Rationale: The text identifies START as "a mnemonic for Simple Triage and Rapid Treatment system."
11. The START system prioritizes treatment needs based on respiratory, perfusion, and what other observation?
Rationale: The document states that START "offers a quick and efficient way to prioritize victims' treatment needs based on respiratory, perfusion, and mental status observations."
12. The START system should not take more than how long per patient to complete?
Rationale: The text advises, "It should not take you more than 30 seconds per patient to complete."
13. In the START category definitions, "Minor or minimal" victims are those who can walk, and their care may be delayed for up to:
Rationale: The "START category" section defines "Minor or minimal" victims as those "...who can get up and walk... and care may be delayed for up to 3 hours."
14. Which START category includes victims who are unresponsive and cannot follow simple commands?
Rationale: The "START category" section defines "Immediate" as: "Victims who are unresponsive and cannot follow simple commands."
15. In the METTAG system used by START, what color tag is for "Potentially serious injuries, but are stable enough to wait"?
Rationale: The section "The tags of START triage system" defines "Yellow" as: "Potentially serious injuries, but are stable enough to wait a short while for medical treatment."
16. What color tag is given to victims who are "Dead or still with life signs but injuries are incompatible with survival in austere conditions"?
Rationale: The section "The tags of START triage system" defines "Black" as: "Dead or still with life signs but injuries are incompatible with survival in austere conditions."
17. In START: Step 1, what does the triage officer instruct all patients who can walk to do?
Rationale: "START: Step 1" states: "Triage officer announces that all patients that can walk should get up and walk to a designated area for eventual secondary triage."
18. In START: Step 2, if a patient is apneic (not breathing), what is the first action?
Rationale: "START: Step 2" says, "If apneic, open airway." The tagging comes after this action.
19. In START: Step 2, if a patient is apneic and *remains* apneic after the airway is opened, what is the correct tag?
Rationale: "START: Step 2" continues, "If patient remains apneic, tag as Black."
20. In START: Step 2, if an apneic patient *starts* breathing after the airway is opened, what is the correct tag?
Rationale: "START: Step 2" concludes, "If patient starts breathing, tag as Red."
21. In START: Step 3, a breathing patient with a respiratory rate greater than 30/minute should be tagged:
Rationale: "START: Step 3" instructs: "Assess respiratory rate... If >30, tag patient as Red."
22. In START: Step 4, a patient with a respiratory rate < 30 but a capillary refill > 2 seconds should be tagged:
Rationale: "START: Step 4" instructs: "Assess capillary refill... If > 2 seconds, tag as Red."
23. In START: Step 5, a patient with RR < 30, cap refill < 2 sec, but who is *unable* to obey commands should be tagged:
Rationale: "START: Step 5" instructs: "Assess mental status... If unable to obey commands, tag as Red."
24. Following the START algorithm, a non-ambulatory patient with RR 24, cap refill < 2 sec, and who *is able* to obey commands should be tagged:
Rationale: The patient is non-ambulatory (Step 1), breathing (Step 2), has RR < 30 (Step 3), cap refill < 2 sec (Step 4), and is able to obey commands. "START: Step 5" concludes: "If able to obey commands, tag as Yellow."
25. Which of the following is listed as an "Intervention for Immediate"?
Rationale: At the end of the START steps, the text lists "Interventions for Immediate: Airway control, Bleeding control, Treatment for shock."
26. The "ABCD Scoring system" is designed for assessment of patients with symptoms of:
Rationale: The title for this section explicitly states: "ABCD Scoring system for assessment by Health care professional at Emergency department in patient with symptoms of COVID-19."
27. In the ABCD system, which variable scores a "1" if body temperature is >100.4 Fahrenheit?
Rationale: The table for the ABCD system shows the variable "Body temperature" with the criteria ">100.4 Fahrenheit" corresponding to a score of "1".
28. Which of the following is NOT listed as a "Comorbidity" that scores a 1 in the ABCD system?
Rationale: The "Comorbidities" listed in the table are COPD/Smoker, Cancer, Hypertension & Chronic heart disease, Chronic renal disease, and Diabetes mellitus. Asthma is not listed.
29. In the ABCD system, a total score of 4-8 is classified as "Moderate" (Yellow) and requires what treatment?
Rationale: The table at the bottom of page 5 shows that a score of "4-8" is "Yellow" and "Moderate," requiring "Active treatment: Semi-critical care & Oxygen supplementation."
30. A score of >8 in the ABCD system is classified as "Severe" (Red) and requires what treatment?
Rationale: The table at the bottom of page 5 shows that a score of ">8" is "Red" and "Severe," requiring "Urgent treatment: Critical & Intensive care."
31. True or False: Triage is one of the *first* functions performed at the scene of a multiple-casualty incident.
Rationale: The "History of triage" section states, "Triage is one of the first functions performed at the scene of a multiple-casualty incident..."
32. True or False: According to the text, health care workers should only perform triage *outside* the facility, not inside.
Rationale: The introduction states, "In addition to triage occurring outside the facility, health care workers should also perform triage inside the facility..."
33. True or False: Surge capacity management strategies include managing space, staff, and materials.
Rationale: The section "Manage surge capacity" explicitly says, "Strategies used to manage surge capacity include managing space, staff, and materials."
34. True or False: Secondary triage occurs immediately upon arrival of the first EMS crew and provides the basic categorization.
Rationale: This describes *Primary Triage*. The text states Secondary Triage "Occurs once the patients are moved from the scene to the triage unit" and "is designed to reevaluate the patient categorization."
35. True or False: The START system is recommended for adults and children of all ages and weights.
Rationale: The text specifies that START "is recommended for adults and can also be used for children who are older than 8 years of age and greater than 45 kg in weight."
36. True or False: According to the text, there is universal agreement regarding the design of triage tags.
Rationale: The section "The tags of START triage system" clearly states: "There is no universal agreement regarding the design of such tags."
37. True or False: In START Step 1, all ambulatory patients (those who can walk) are initially tagged as Green.
Rationale: "START: Step 1" says, "All ambulatory patients are initially tagged as Green."
38. True or False: According to the START algorithm, victims in the "Expectant" (Black) category should receive palliative care and pain relief.
Rationale: The "Triage Categories" box for "Expectant - Black triage tag color" states: "Palliative care and pain relief should be provided."
39. True or False: In the ABCD scoring system, an age of 50 or younger gives a score of "0".
Rationale: The table for the "ABCD Scoring system" shows that for the "Age (Years)" variable, the range "0-50" corresponds to a "Score" of "0".
40. True or False: In the ABCD system, a score of 0-4 is classified as "Moderate" (Yellow).
Rationale: The table at the bottom of page 5 clearly shows that a "Total Score" of "0-4" is classified as "Green" and "Mild," requiring "Observation: Symptomatic treatment in ward."
