1. Which type of defibrillator waveform flows in two directions, is more effective, and requires a lower energy level?

Rationale: Biphasic technology incorporates two-way current flow. It is more effective and safe, and needs low joules (150-200 J) to deliver a shock.

2. Which type of collar is a stiff, two-piece foam collar that supports the lower jaw and extends down to the upper thoracic spine, often used after surgery?

Rationale: A Philadelphia collar is a stiff foam collar composed of 2 pieces. It covers more area and puts greater restrictions on motion, making it common after cervical spine surgery.

3. True or False: When administering IV Sodium Bicarbonate, it is critical to flush the line before and after administration.

Rationale: True. You must flush the line before and after administration of sodium bicarbonate. It is incompatible with many other emergency drugs, such as calcium chloride and epinephrine.

4. What is the standard paper speed for recording an ECG?

Rationale: The standard paper speed is usually 25 mm/s. The standard calibration (amplitude) is 10 mm/mV.

5. What does the T wave represent on an ECG tracing?

Rationale: The T wave in an ECG is the result of the relaxation of cardiac muscle, also known as ventricular repolarization.

6. Which inhalation device is mechanical, features no gas propellant, and does NOT require a battery or power to operate?

Rationale: The soft mist inhaler is rotated by hand to build tension in a spring, which forms a soft mist. It features no gas propellant and no need for battery/power to operate.

7. True or False: Electrode packages should be opened at the start of the shift to save time, even if they aren't being used immediately.

Rationale: False. Avoid opening the electrode packages until just before using to prevent the gel from drying out, which can decrease conduction.

8. Cardioversion is the recommended treatment for which of these rhythms?

Rationale: Synchronized cardioversion is recommended for unstable atrial tachycardia, atrial fibrillation, atrial flutter, and *ventricular tachycardia with a pulse*. VF and pulseless VT require defibrillation.

9. True or False: For the best ECG signal, electrodes should be placed directly on bony prominences and hairy locations.

Rationale: False. Avoid placing electrodes on bony prominences, hairy locations, areas for defibrillator pads, or areas for chest compression.

10. Where is the V1 chest lead placed for a 12-lead ECG?

Rationale: V1 (white/red lead) is placed in the fourth intercostal space, just to the right of the sternum.

11. Who is considered the leader of the log roll team and is in control of the roll?

Rationale: The head hold person must be a registered nurse or medical officer; this person manages cervical spine alignment and is in control of the roll.

12. True or False: A standard 12-lead ECG uses 12 electrodes placed on the patient's body.

Rationale: False. A standard ECG uses 10 lead wires with electrodes at the ends (4 placed on the limbs, and 6 placed on the chest) to produce 12 electrical views (leads).

13. In a standard five-lead system, where is the LA (black) lead placed?

Rationale: The LA (black) lead is placed below the left clavicle, 2nd Intercostal Space (ICS), Mid-Clavicular Line (MCL).

14. What is the MAIN principle of the log roll maneuver?

Rationale: The log roll is a maneuver used to move a patient *without flexing the spinal column* to maintain alignment and prevent further injury.

15. What is the standard sterno-apical placement for defibrillator paddles?

Rationale: Standard sterno-apical placement is one paddle to the right of the sternum below the clavicle, and the other at the apex, in the 5th intercostal space, mid-axillary line.

16. The "PAAD" acronym for using an AED stands for:

Rationale: The technique for using an AED is PAAD: P- Power on, A- Attach pads, A - Analyze, D - Defibrillate.

17. Which of the following is an indication for performing a log roll?

Rationale: Indications include examination of the patient's back, cervical collar care, pressure care, and transporting a casualty with suspected spinal injury.

18. True or False: Routine "stripping" or "milking" of chest tubes is highly recommended to prevent clots.

Rationale: False. Routine chest tube stripping or milking is *not* recommended as it can cause transient high negative pressures and potential lung entrapment.

19. The "hip person" is primarily responsible for ensuring the:

Rationale: This person is responsible for ensuring the lower spine is not twisted during the roll.

20. What is "tidaling" in a chest tube's water seal chamber?

Rationale: Tidaling is the normal movement (rising and falling) of the fluid in the water seal tube that corresponds with the patient's breathing (up on inspiration, down on expiration).

21. True or False: For consistency and ease of use, pediatric and adult crash carts should be the same color and style.

Rationale: False. The pediatric cart should be separate and distinct from the adult cart (e.g., different style, different color) to prevent errors.

22. Which of the following is an indication for nebulizer therapy?

Rationale: Indications for nebulizer therapy include Asthma, Cystic fibrosis, COPD, and other respiratory disorders.

23. True or False: Nebulizers are only used to deliver antibiotics.

Rationale: False. Nebulizers are most commonly used for bronchodilators, but can also deliver anticholinergics, corticosteroids, antibiotics, and antifungals.

24. What does the P wave represent on an ECG tracing?

Rationale: Electrical stimulation of the atria produces the P wave in an ECG and results in coordinated mechanical contraction (atrial systole).

25. True or False: A patient wearing a cervical collar should be encouraged to sit in soft, low chairs to maximize comfort.

Rationale: False. Avoid sitting in soft, low chairs. This can affect posture and put extra pressure on the neck. A firm chair is recommended.

26. Which type of nebulizer is also commonly called an "atomizer" and uses compressed air?

Rationale: The most commonly used nebulizers are jet nebulizers, which are also called "atomizers". They use compressed air or oxygen to turn liquid medicine into an aerosol.

27. True or False: A primary disadvantage of ultrasonic wave nebulizers is that they are very loud.

Rationale: False. An *advantage* of ultrasonic wave nebulizers is that they are almost silent. Disadvantages include liquid waste and potential heating of the medication.

28. Which of the following is a potential complication of nebulizer therapy?

Rationale: Complications include irritation of mucosa, bronchospasm, dyspnea, airway burns, and infection from contaminated equipment. Tachycardia (not bradycardia) is also a common side effect.

29. What is the normal heart rate generated by the sinus node under normal conditions?

Rationale: The sinus node generates an electrical stimulus regularly, 60 to 100 times per minute under normal conditions.

30. What does the QRS complex represent on an ECG tracing?

Rationale: Electrical stimulation of the ventricles produces the QRS complex on an ECG and results in coordinated ventricular contraction (ventricular systole).

31. What is the standard recommended dosage of Aspirin to treat Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS)?

Rationale: The standard recommended aspirin dosage to treat ACS is 160 to 325 mg, typically chewed to speed absorption.

32. Which inotropic drug increases myocardial contractility *without* a significant change in blood pressure, making it a good choice for heart failure with ischemia?

Rationale: Dobutamine increases myocardial contractility & cardiac output without a significant change in blood pressure. It is generally considered the inotrope of choice in patients with myocardial ischemia.

33. Where is the V4 chest lead placed for a 12-lead ECG?

Rationale: V4 (white/brown lead) is placed in the fifth intercostal space, mid-clavicular line.

34. A "renal dose" of Dopamine, which causes vasodilation in renal and mesenteric beds, is typically:

Rationale: At low rates of infusion (< 5 mcg/kg/min), Dopamine affects dopaminergic receptors, causing vasodilation in the renal and other vascular beds, thus increasing urine output.

35. True or False: A standard 12-lead ECG uses 12 electrodes placed on the patient's body.

Rationale: False. A standard ECG uses 10 lead wires with electrodes at the ends (4 placed on the limbs, and 6 placed on the chest) to produce 12 electrical views (leads).

36. A three-electrode monitoring system typically allows viewing of which leads?

Rationale: The three-electrode system produces limited selections of leads I, II, or III with only a single lead viewed on the screen at one time.

37. True or False: It is crucial to remove any transdermal medication patches from the patient's chest before defibrillation.

Rationale: True. Medication patches must be removed as they can block the electrical current and cause burns to the patient's skin.

38. True or False: An AED should be applied to any patient who is responsive but complaining of severe chest pain.

Rationale: False. An AED should be applied only to *Unresponsive, non-breathing, and pulseless* patients.

39. A one-bottle drainage system acts as both the drainage collection chamber and the:

Rationale: In a one-bottle system, the bottle acts as both the collection chamber and the water seal (the long tube is immersed 2 cm under water).

40. In a standard five-lead system, where is the LL (red) lead placed?

Rationale: The LL (red) lead is placed on the left lower rib cage, 8th Intercostal Space (ICS), Mid-Clavicular Line (MCL).

41. Which of the following items would be found in Drawer 2 (Breathing and Airway)?

Rationale: Drawer 2 contains the intubation tray, which includes laryngoscope handles, blades (straight and curved), and other airway supplies.

42. In a standard five-lead system, where is the RA (white) lead placed?

Rationale: The RA (white) lead is placed below the right clavicle, 2nd Intercostal Space (ICS), Mid-Clavicular Line (MCL).

43. What is the FIRST choice drug for treating symptomatic bradycardia?

Rationale: Atropine is the first choice drug for symptomatic bradycardia. It works by increasing the heart rate through its anticholinergic effect.

44. A normal QRS complex width is less than:

Rationale: The normal width of a QRS complex is less than 0.12 seconds (3 small squares).

45. True or False: A sign of tension pneumothorax is tracheal deviation towards the AFFECTED side.

Rationale: False. In a tension pneumothorax, pressure builds up on the affected side, pushing the trachea and heart *away* from the injury, towards the *unaffected* side.

46. What is considered the natural pacemaker of the heart, generating the initial electrical stimulus?

Rationale: The Sinoatrial (SA) node is a small mass of specialized tissue in the right atrium that generates the electrical stimulus regularly, acting as the heart's natural pacemaker.

47. Drawer 3 (Circulation and IV supplies) typically contains:

Rationale: Drawer 3 contains IV supplies such as 3-way stopcocks, alcohol swabs, various sizes of syringes, blood tubes, and IV start kits.

48. Which type of collar is flexible, made of foam, and typically used as a transition *after* a more rigid collar?

Rationale: Soft collars are flexible and made of foam. They are usually worn after a more rigid collar to help gradually transition to not wearing a brace.

49. True or False: Skin under the cervical collar should be inspected for redness and breakdown at least twice a day.

Rationale: True. Always assess skin integrity, especially over bony prominences. You should inspect the patient's skin under and around the collar at least twice a day.

50. A major potential complication of performing cardioversion on a patient in atrial fibrillation is:

Rationale: Atrial tachydysrhythmias (like A-fib) predispose the patient to the development of microemboli (clots) in the atria. Cardioversion can release these clots, causing pulmonary or cerebral emboli (stroke).

51. According to the typical organization, Drawer 1 of a crash cart contains:

Rationale: Drawers are organized top to bottom. Drawer 1 typically contains Adult Medications (e.g., Epinephrine, Amiodarone, Atropine).

52. True or False: The head hold person must be a registered nurse or medical officer.

Rationale: True. The document specifies: "Head hold person: Must be a registered nurse or medical officer".

53. The "chest person" on the log roll team should ideally be:

Rationale: The "chest person" should be the tallest person in the team, who places hands over the patient's shoulder and lower back.

54. What is the MAIN purpose of a chest tube drainage system?

Rationale: The system is designed to evacuate fluid, blood, and air from the pleural space and to restore negative pressure, promoting re-expansion of the lung.

55. A "cardiac dose" of Dopamine, which improves contractility, is typically:

Rationale: At intermediate rates (5-10 mcg/kg/min), Dopamine affects beta1-adrenoceptors, resulting in improved myocardial contractility.

56. True or False: Using the tools on a crash cart requires advanced medical skills, such as ACLS certification.

Rationale: True. Using the tools on a crash cart requires advanced medical skills and certification in Advanced Cardiac Life Support (ACLS).

57. True or False: It is safe for a patient to drive a car while wearing a cervical collar.

Rationale: False. Instruct the patient not to drive or operate equipment while in the collar, as they cannot turn their head to check blind spots.

58. What is the key difference between defibrillation and cardioversion?

Rationale: Cardioversion is a *synchronized* electrical shock. The defibrillator is set to "sync" mode to sense the R wave and deliver the shock at a specific point, avoiding the vulnerable T wave period. Defibrillation is unsynchronized.

59. What piece of equipment is typically found on the TOP of the crash cart?

Rationale: The top of the crash cart typically holds the portable monitor/defibrillator unit, suction apparatus, and check sheets.

60. Which system uses a small, battery-operated transmitter to send the ECG signal via radiofrequency, allowing the patient to be mobile?

Rationale: In telemetry monitoring, no direct wire is needed. Electrodes connect to a small battery-operated transmitter, and the ECG is sent by radiofrequency signals.

61. What is the FIRST thing you should do if a chest tube is accidentally dislodged from the patient's chest?

Rationale: If the tube is dislodged, immediately apply an occlusive dressing to the tube site (to prevent air from entering the pleural space) and notify the physician.

62. True or False: To maintain alignment, a pillow may be inserted between the patient's legs during a log roll.

Rationale: True. A pillow may be inserted between the patient's legs to maintain alignment of the hips and spine.

63. True or False: During nebulizer treatment, the patient should be instructed to breathe as quickly and shallowly as possible.

Rationale: False. The patient should be instructed to breathe slowly and deeply until all the medication is nebulized to ensure good inhalation.

64. A normal PR interval is between:

Rationale: The normal PR interval is 0.12-0.20 seconds (3-5 small squares). It represents the time taken for the impulse to travel from the SA node to the ventricles.

65. A five-electrode system adds an exploring "chest" electrode, which allows the nurse to view:

Rationale: The five-electrode monitor adds an exploring "chest" electrode that allows one to obtain any one of the six chest leads and the six limb leads, providing all the capabilities of a 12-lead ECG machine.

66. A large volume nebulizer is MAINLY used for:

Rationale: A large volume nebulizer is primarily used to administer solution to hydrate the airway and reduce inflammation. A small volume nebulizer is used to administer drugs.

67. True or False: The electrical impulse speeds up as it travels through the atrioventricular (AV) node.

Rationale: False. The impulse travels to the AV node, where impulses are *slowed down* for a very short period. This delay allows the atria to empty into the ventricles before the ventricles contract.

68. True or False: The goal of defibrillation is to pass an electric current through the heart to *repolarize* all the muscle fibers simultaneously.

Rationale: False. The electric current results in *depolarization* of the myocardial muscle fibers. This disruption of chaotic impulses allows the heart's natural pacemaker (SA node) to resume control.

69. True or False: The normal electrical axis of the heart ranges between -30° and +90°.

Rationale: True. Normally, the axis is down and to the patient's left. The normal electrical axis ranges between -30° to +90°.

70. An oxygen cylinder on the crash cart should be replaced when the tank has less than:

Rationale: Oxygen cylinders are replaced when the tank has less than 500 psi.

71. What is the primary purpose of a cardiac monitor?

Rationale: The primary purpose is to continuously display the cardiac electrocardiogram (EKG) tracing, allowing for prompt identification of rhythm changes.

72. Which type of monitoring system limits patient mobility because the patient is linked directly to the monitor via a cable?

Rationale: Hard-wire monitors require the patient to be linked directly to the cardiac monitor. Patients using this system are usually confined to bed rest or allowed up at the bedside only.

73. True or False: Normal intrapleural pressure is positive during expiration, at approximately +4 cmH2O.

Rationale: False. Normal intrapleural pressure is *negative*, measuring approximately -4 cmH2O during expiration and -8 cmH2O at the end of inspiration.

74. The leader (head hold person) initiates the coordinated roll after counting to:

Rationale: After the head person counts 1, 2, 3, a coordinated roll of the patient... is performed.

75. True or False: During a log roll, the team members at the patient's side roll the patient *away* from themselves.

Rationale: False. A coordinated roll of the patient *towards* the hip & chest person is performed. This allows for better control and for the 5th person to place a backboard.

76. What is the primary purpose of a crash cart?

Rationale: A crash cart is a portable trolley containing all equipment, supplies, and drugs required for cardiopulmonary resuscitation and emergency care.

77. True or False: Log rolling must always be performed immediately, even if it takes precedence over life-saving procedures like securing an airway.

Rationale: False. The application of definitive immobilization devices should *not* take precedence over life-saving procedures, such as the need to obtain and secure an airway.

78. What is the primary vasopressor used in the cardiac arrest algorithm (e.g., for VF/Pulseless VT)?

Rationale: Epinephrine is a cardiac stimulant and vasopressor indicated for cardiac arrest. The standard dose is 1 mg IV every 3-5 minutes.

79. Salbutamol and Terbutaline are examples of which class of nebulizer drugs?

Rationale: Nebulizer drugs can be Beta2 agonists (for example salbutamol, terbutaline).

80. At high "pressor" doses (>10 mcg/kg/min), Dopamine primarily stimulates alpha-adrenoceptors, resulting in:

Rationale: At higher rates of infusion (>10 mcg/kg/min), Dopamine affects alpha-adrenoceptors, with consequent vasoconstrictor effects and a rise in blood pressure.

81. In the "MONA" therapy for Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS), what does the 'A' stand for?

Rationale: MONA therapy stands for M (Morphine), O (Oxygen), N (Nitroglycerin), A (Aspirin).

82. True or False: When log rolling, the patient's head should be stabilized in a slightly flexed (chin-to-chest) position.

Rationale: False. The head hold person manually stabilizes the patient's head in the *neutral position* with both hands.

83. True or False: A properly fitted cervical collar should be snug, but loose enough to allow the patient to nod their head downward.

Rationale: False. The collar should be snug enough to *keep* the patient from nodding their head downward (flexion). If it's too loose, it won't provide the necessary support.

84. What item is typically found on the BACK of a crash cart?

Rationale: The cardiac board is typically stored on the back of the crash cart. The oxygen cylinder is usually on the side.

85. What is the appropriate patient position to facilitate drainage of a hemothorax?

Rationale: The patient should be positioned in Semi-Fowler's (for pneumothorax) or high Fowler's (for hemothorax) to facilitate drainage. Supine or lateral may also be used during insertion.

86. True or False: A log roll can be safely and effectively performed by a single trained first aider.

Rationale: False. A log roll is best performed using four to six first aiders. It should not be provided when there are not enough personnel to safely perform the technique.

87. According to the typical organization, Drawer 2 of a crash cart contains:

Rationale: Drawer 2 typically contains Breathing and Airway supplies, such as the Ambu bag, oral airways, laryngoscopes, and endotracheal tubes.

88. What is one of the most common and serious complications of long-term cervical collar use?

Rationale: Complications include skin damage, especially at risk for pressure ulcers (on the chin, ears, shoulders, etc.), and a potential *increase* in intracranial pressure.

89. True or False: A soft collar provides more motion restriction than a Philadelphia collar.

Rationale: False. The Philadelphia collar covers more area and is made of stiff foam, so it puts *greater* restrictions on range of motion than a soft collar.

90. True or False: Persistent, continuous bubbling in the water seal chamber indicates the system is working normally.

Rationale: False. Intermittent bubbling (with coughing or exhalation) is normal. *Persistent* or *continuous* bubbling indicates an air leak in the patient's lung or the drainage system.

91. To drain AIR (pneumothorax), where is a chest tube typically inserted?

Rationale: To drain air, the tube is placed high, near the apex of the lung (e.g., 2nd intercostal space, mid-clavicle line) because air rises.

92. In the "MONA" therapy for Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS), what does the 'M' stand for?

Rationale: MONA therapy stands for M (Morphine), O (Oxygen), N (Nitroglycerin), A (Aspirin).

93. Which of these medications is commonly found in the FIRST drawer?

Rationale: Drawer 1 contains adult emergency medications, including Adenosine, Amiodarone, Atropine, Dopamine, Epinephrine, Lidocaine, etc.

94. True or False: Defibrillation must be synchronized with the patient's R wave to be effective.

Rationale: False. *Cardioversion* is synchronized with the R wave. *Defibrillation* is an unsynchronized shock, delivered randomly, because in rhythms like VF, there is no organized R wave to sync to.

95. The physician should be notified if chest tube drainage is suddenly bloody or exceeds:

Rationale: Notify the physician if the drainage is more than 100 cc per hour (or >= 200 ml/hr as noted on Page 5). 100 cc/hr is a standard and safe threshold for reporting.

Rationale: Magnesium Sulfate is indicated for Torsades de Pointes, as well as seizures associated with eclampsia and hypomagnesemia.

97. Drawer 4 (Circulation and tubing) typically contains:

Rationale: Drawer 4 contains IV solutions (Lactated Ringer's, Normal Saline, D5%) and IV tubing (blood tubing, extension tubing, micro/macro drips).

98. True or False: Performing oral care after a nebulizer treatment is important to clean the mouth from the bad taste of medication.

Rationale: True. Oral care should be performed as needed to clean the mouth from the bad taste of medication. This is especially important with corticosteroid inhalations to prevent thrush.

99. A cervical collar is also known as a:

Rationale: A cervical collar, also known as a neck brace, is a medical device used to support a person's neck.

Rationale: Horizontally, one small square (1 mm) = 0.04 seconds. One large square (5 mm) = 0.20 seconds.

101. Lead V1 (or MCL1) is particularly helpful for detecting:

Rationale: Lead V1 or MCL1 is helpful for detecting right bundle branch block and differentiating ventricular ectopy from supraventricular rhythm.

102. What is the MOST effective therapy for the termination of ventricular fibrillation (VF) and ventricular tachydysrhythmias?

Rationale: Defibrillation is the most effective therapy for the termination of ventricular fibrillation and ventricular tachydysrhythmias.

Rationale: The three-bottle system consists of the drainage collection bottle, the water seal bottle, and the addition of a suction control bottle.

Rationale: The oxygen flow should be adjusted to at least 10 L/min but not more than 14 L/min to change the medication into a mist.

106. True or False: The height of an individual is a reliable and accurate method for determining the correct cervical collar size.

Rationale: Calcium Chloride is indicated for acute hyperkalemia, acute hypocalcemia, and calcium channel overdose. It increases cardiac contractility.

109. What is a nebulizer?

Rationale: A nebulizer is a device used to administer medication in the form of a mist inhaled into the lungs.

111. Nitroglycerin is given in ACS primarily for its potent:

Rationale: Nitroglycerin is a potent vasodilator. It relaxes vascular smooth-muscle beds, including coronary arteries, improving blood flow and decreasing cardiac workload.

112. True or False: Morphine is given during a myocardial infarction (MI) to relieve pain and reduce myocardial oxygen requirements.

Rationale: True. Morphine relieves pain, relieves pulmonary congestion, lowers myocardial oxygen requirement, and reduces anxiety.

114. Monophasic defibrillators deliver a single shock with energy levels typically ranging from:

Rationale: Monophasic machines need high joules to deliver a shock (from 200 to 360 J).

116. The log rolling procedure is implemented for all patients with a suspected injury to:

Rationale: The procedure is implemented on all patients suspected of having a possible spinal injury, prior to the cervical spine and/or thoracolumbar spine being cleared.

117. What is transthoracic impedance?

Rationale: Transthoracic impedance is the resistance of the thorax to the flow of current. Poor technique (like insufficient gel or pressure) increases impedance.

118. What is the recommended patient position for nebulizer therapy to improve lung expansion?

Rationale: The patient should be put in a semi-Fowler's position to improve lung expansion.

119. True or False: The crash cart and defibrillator must be checked at the beginning of every nursing shift.

Rationale: True. At the beginning of a nursing shift, the responsible nurse should check that the defibrillator is charged and check the cart contents against the checklist.

120. To drain FLUID (hemothorax), where is a chest tube typically inserted?

Rationale: To drain fluid, the tube is placed low, near the base of the lung (e.g., 4th to 5th intercostal spaces, mid-axillary line) because fluid pools with gravity.
